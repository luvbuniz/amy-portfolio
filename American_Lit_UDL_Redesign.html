Curriculum Overview & UDL Accommodations

Integrated Support Structures
FOUNDATION

This curriculum integrates rigorous literary analysis with accessible, high-engagement strategies. The following Universal Design for Learning (UDL) supports are built into every week to ensure equitable access.

Assessment Methods
  • Traditional Writing
  • Visual Representations (Graphic Organizers)
  • Verbal Explanations
  • Creative Interpretations
  • Physical Demonstrations
Support Structures
  • Sentence starters always available
  • Visual anchor charts displayed
  • Partner support options
  • Extended time as needed
  • Choice in expression format
Technology & Classroom Environment
43" Screen Optimization

Use large, clear fonts (24pt+), high contrast visuals, and limit text density per slide. Include images/diagrams for all concepts.

Environment

Movement breaks built in. Quiet processing spaces available. Timer displays for all station activities.

Perseverance and Encouragement

"Finding Strength in Rhyme"
WEEK 1
Focus Text & Objectives

"Don't Quit" by Edgar A. Guest

  • Analyze the effect of AABB rhyme scheme on poem structure.
  • Identify and interpret central metaphors regarding perseverance.
  • Apply grammar rules to identify fragments vs. complete sentences.
Literary Devices
Metaphor: Life as a journey ("twists and turns").
Imagery: Visualizing hope ("silver tint of clouds").
Rhyme Scheme: AABB (predictable, memorable rhythm).
Lesson Flow (50 min)
5mOpening RoutineEmotion Check-in: "Don't Quit" image association & visual scale.
15mSlide PresentationAuthor Context (People's Poet). Concept: Metaphor & Rhyme. Guided Reading.
10mDiscussion"How does the rhythm affect the message?" Personal connection wheel.
10mClasswork StationsSee below.
10mGrammar & HomeworkFragments vs. Sentences (Car analogy). Prep options.
Classwork Stations
Rhyme
Mark AABB pattern.
Metaphor
Write 4-line stanza.
Rhythm
Clapping meter.
Analysis
Label imagery.
Homework Options (Choose 2)
  • Write AABB poem about perseverance.
  • Analyze rhyme scheme in 3 other poems.
  • Creative story using metaphors.
  • Illustrated poem with rhyme marked.
UDL Strategy: Color-code rhyming lines for visual support. Use clapping to demonstrate meter/rhythm kinesthetically.
