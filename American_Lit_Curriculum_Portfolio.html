Curriculum Overview

This 27-week scope and sequence is designed to guide students through a diverse exploration of American Literature. The curriculum integrates rigorous literary analysis with accessible, high-engagement strategies, ensuring all learners can access complex texts.

Instructional Methodology

  • UDL Framework: Multiple means of representation, expression, and engagement built into every lesson.
  • Routine-Based: Predictable 50-minute structure to reduce cognitive load.
  • Multimodal: Emphasis on visual, auditory, and kinesthetic learning.

Standard Class Structure (50 Min)

5 min Opening Routine & Emotion Check-in
15 min Slide Presentation & Literary Analysis
10 min Guided Discussion & Analysis
10 min Classwork Stations & Application
5 min Grammar Focus
5 min Homework Prep

Differentiation & Accommodations

These strategies are integrated throughout the 27-week curriculum to ensure equitable access for all learners.

Assessment Methods

  • Traditional Writing
  • Visual Representations
  • Verbal Explanations
  • Creative Interpretations
  • Physical Demonstrations

Support Structures

  • Sentence starters always available
  • Visual anchor charts displayed
  • Partner support options
  • Extended time as needed
  • Choice in expression format
  • Movement breaks built-in

Technology Optimization (43" Screen)

Use large, clear fonts (24pt+), high contrast visuals, and limit text density per slide. Utilize timer displays for all station activities to support executive function.
